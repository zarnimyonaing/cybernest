Fake Websites & Online Scams

Chapter Objective: Learn to identify fake websites and online scams, understand how they operate, and protect yourself from becoming a victim.

Understanding Online Scams

Online scams are fraudulent schemes designed to trick users into revealing personal information, sending money, or downloading malware. Scammers create convincing fake websites and use psychological manipulation to exploit trust and urgency.

  • Cryptocurrency scam sites
  • Fake insurance websites
E-commerce
  • Fake online stores

Government/Official
  • Fake government portals
  • Fraudulent tax sites

  • Phony official notifications
Romance/Dating
  • Fake dating platforms

Red Flags for Fake Websites

Visual and Technical Warning Signs:
  • URL Inconsistencies: Misspellings, extra characters, or wrong domains
  • Poor Design: Low-quality graphics, inconsistent layouts, spelling errors
  • Missing Security: No HTTPS encryption on login/payment pages
  • Pressure Tactics: Urgent countdown timers, limited-time offers
  • Too Good to Be True: Unrealistic prices or guaranteed returns
  • Limited Contact Info: No phone number, address, or support details
  • Payment Red Flags: Only accepting wire transfers or cryptocurrency
  • Suspicious Certificates: Invalid or missing SSL certificates

How to Verify Website Legitimacy

URL Verification Techniques:

URL Analysis Steps:
  1. Check the domain: Look for official domain names (not variations)
  2. Look for HTTPS: Secure sites should use SSL encryption
  3. Examine the path: Be suspicious of long, complex URLs
  4. Verify spelling: Check for typos in domain names
  5. Use WHOIS lookup: Research domain registration information
  6. Check domain age: Very new domains may be suspicious

Website Authentication Methods:

Verification Tools:
  • Google Safe Browsing checker
  • Norton Safe Web
  • McAfee SiteAdvisor
  • Trustpilot reviews
  • Better Business Bureau ratings
Manual Verification:
  • Search for company reviews
  • Check social media presence
  • Verify physical address
  • Test customer service contacts
  • Look for professional certifications

Common Online Scam Scenarios

1. Too-Good-to-Be-True Offers

The Scam: Extremely low prices, guaranteed investment returns, or exclusive deals

Red Flags: Pressure to act immediately, requires upfront payment, no return policy

Protection: Research market prices, read reviews, verify seller legitimacy

2. Advance Fee Scams

The Scam: Request for small payment to receive larger sum (prizes, loans, inheritances)

Red Flags: Unsolicited contact, requires fees upfront, asks for personal information

Protection: Never pay fees to receive money, verify through official channels

3. Fake Support Scams

The Scam: Pop-ups claiming computer infection, fake tech support sites

Red Flags: Unsolicited warnings, requests for remote access, immediate payment demands

Protection: Never call numbers from pop-ups, contact companies directly

E-commerce Scam Prevention

Safe Online Shopping Practices:

Before Making a Purchase:
  • Research the seller and read reviews from multiple sources
  • Verify contact information and return policies
  • Check if the website accepts secure payment methods
  • Look for professional certifications and trust seals
  • Compare prices with other retailers
  • Read terms and conditions carefully
  • Check shipping costs and delivery timeframes

Payment Security:

Safe Payment Methods:
  • Credit cards (better fraud protection)
  • PayPal and reputable payment services
  • Secure payment gateways
  • Digital wallets with fraud protection
Avoid:
  • Wire transfers
  • Cryptocurrency payments
  • Gift cards as payment
  • Direct bank transfers to strangers

Social Engineering Tactics

Psychological Manipulation Techniques:

  • Urgency: "Limited time offer," "Act now or lose out"
  • Authority: Impersonating government agencies or well-known companies
  • Scarcity: "Only 3 left in stock," "Exclusive opportunity"
  • Social Proof: Fake testimonials and reviews
  • Fear: "Your account will be closed," "Legal action will be taken"
  • Greed: Promise of easy money or extraordinary deals

What to Do If You've Been Scammed

Immediate Response Steps:
  1. Stop all contact: Don't send more money or information
  2. Document everything: Save emails, screenshots, and receipts
  3. Contact financial institutions: Report fraudulent charges immediately
  4. Change passwords: Update all compromised account credentials
  5. Run security scans: Check for malware on your devices
  6. Report the scam: File complaints with relevant authorities
  7. Monitor accounts: Watch for additional unauthorized activity
  8. Seek help: Contact consumer protection agencies

  • Turn on safe browsing warnings
  • Use ad blockers to prevent malicious ads
  • Keep browsers updated with latest security patches
  • Enable download protection scanning
  • Use reputable security extensions
  • Configure privacy settings appropriately
