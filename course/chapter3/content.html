Phishing

Chapter Objective: Learn to identify, avoid, and respond to phishing attacks that attempt to steal your personal information and credentials.

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers impersonate legitimate organizations to trick victims into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data. The term comes from "fishing" because attackers cast a wide net hoping to catch unsuspecting victims.

Types of Phishing Attacks

Email Phishing

Mass emails sent to many recipients, often impersonating banks, social media, or popular services.

Spear Phishing

Targeted attacks on specific individuals or organizations using personalized information.

Vishing (Voice)

Phone calls pretending to be from legitimate organizations requesting sensitive information.

Smishing (SMS)

Text messages containing malicious links or requesting personal information.

Common Phishing Tactics

Red Flags to Watch For:

Suspicious Characteristics:
  • Urgent language: "Act now!" "Account will be closed!" "Immediate action required!"
  • Generic greetings: "Dear Customer" instead of your actual name
  • Suspicious sender: Email addresses that don't match the organization
  • Grammar/spelling errors: Poor writing quality in official communications
  • Suspicious links: Hover to see if URLs match the supposed organization
  • Unexpected attachments: Files you weren't expecting
  • Requests for sensitive info: Asking for passwords, SSN, or financial details

How to Identify Phishing Emails

Check the Sender:

  • Look at the full email address, not just the display name
  • Be suspicious of slight misspellings (amaz0n.com instead of amazon.com)
  • Verify the sender through official channels if unsure

Examine Links Carefully:

Link Inspection Technique:
  1. Hover over links without clicking to see the actual URL
  2. Look for suspicious domains or URL shorteners
  3. Check for HTTPS on login pages
  4. Be wary of URLs with strange characters or numbers

Analyze the Content:

  • Check for personalization - legitimate companies usually use your name
  • Look for professional formatting and branding
  • Be suspicious of urgent requests for action
  • Verify any claims by contacting the organization directly

Common Phishing Scenarios

Fake Bank Alerts

Claims of suspicious account activity requiring immediate login to verify identity.

Social Media Security

Fake notifications about login attempts or account compromises on social platforms.

Package Delivery

Fake delivery notifications requiring personal information to reschedule or track packages.

Protection Strategies

Email Security Settings:

  • Enable spam filters in your email client
  • Use email clients with phishing protection
  • Be cautious with email previews
  • Don't automatically download images

Safe Practices:

Do:
  • Verify suspicious emails through official channels
  • Type URLs directly into browser
  • Use bookmarks for frequently visited sites
  • Keep software updated
  • Use multi-factor authentication
Don't:
  • Click links in suspicious emails
  • Download unexpected attachments
  • Provide personal info via email
  • Trust urgent requests
  • Use the same password everywhere

What to Do If You Fall Victim

Immediate Response:
  1. Change passwords immediately for affected accounts
  2. Contact financial institutions if banking info was compromised
  3. Monitor accounts for unauthorized activity
  4. Report the incident to relevant authorities
  5. Scan your device for malware
  6. Enable fraud alerts on credit reports
  7. Document everything for potential investigation

Reporting Phishing

Help protect others by reporting phishing attempts:

  • Forward phishing emails to your organization's IT security team
  • Report to the Anti-Phishing Working Group (reportphishing@apwg.org)
  • Use your email provider's phishing report feature
  • Report to relevant government agencies
Remember: When in doubt, don't click! Verify through official channels. It's better to be overly cautious than to become a victim.
