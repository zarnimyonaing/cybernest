ID Theft / Personal Info Security

Chapter Objective: Learn how to protect your personal information and prevent identity theft in both digital and physical environments.

What is Identity Theft?

Identity theft occurs when someone unlawfully obtains and uses your personal information without permission to commit fraud or other crimes. This can result in financial loss, damaged credit, and significant time and effort to restore your identity.

Types of Personal Information at Risk

Primary Identifiers
  • Social Security Number (SSN)
  • Driver's License Number
  • Passport Number
  • Date of Birth
  • Full Legal Name
Financial Information
  • Bank Account Numbers
  • Credit Card Numbers
  • Investment Account Info
  • Insurance Policy Numbers
  • Tax Information

Common Methods of Identity Theft

Dumpster Diving

Searching through trash for personal documents

Shoulder Surfing

Watching you enter passwords or PINs

Mail Theft

Stealing mail containing sensitive information

Digital Identity Theft Methods

  • Phishing: Fraudulent emails requesting personal information
  • Data Breaches: Unauthorized access to company databases
  • Social Engineering: Manipulating people to reveal information
  • Skimming: Stealing credit card data at point of sale
  • Malware: Software designed to steal personal data
  • Public Wi-Fi Attacks: Intercepting data on unsecured networks

Warning Signs of Identity Theft

Be Alert For:
  • Unexplained charges on bank or credit card statements
  • Bills or statements that don't arrive as expected
  • Unexpected bills for services you didn't sign up for
  • Denial of credit for no apparent reason
  • Calls or letters about purchases you didn't make
  • Medical bills for services you didn't receive
  • IRS notification of duplicate tax returns
  • Missing mail or unexpected mail

Protection Strategies

Safeguarding Physical Documents

Secure Storage:
  • Use a locked filing cabinet for important papers
  • Store documents in a fireproof safe
  • Keep only necessary items in your wallet
  • Use a safety deposit box for very important documents
Proper Disposal:
  • Shred documents containing personal information
  • Destroy expired credit cards completely
  • Wipe electronic devices before disposal
  • Use cross-cut shredders for maximum security

Digital Protection Measures

Online Safety Practices:
  • Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts
  • Enable two-factor authentication where available
  • Regularly monitor your credit reports
  • Be cautious about sharing personal information online
  • Use secure websites (HTTPS) for sensitive transactions
  • Keep software and operating systems updated
  • Use reputable antivirus software

Social Media Privacy

Social Media Best Practices:
  • Limit personal information in profiles
  • Use privacy settings to control who sees your posts
  • Avoid posting your location in real-time
  • Don't share photos of important documents
  • Be selective about friend/connection requests
  • Avoid sharing financial information or passwords
  • Think before posting - it may be permanent

Credit Monitoring and Protection

Regular Monitoring:

  • Check credit reports from all three bureaus annually
  • Review bank and credit card statements monthly
  • Monitor your credit score regularly
  • Set up account alerts for transactions

Credit Protection Services:

Fraud Alerts

Place alerts on your credit files to be notified of new account applications

Credit Freezes

Prevent new accounts from being opened without your permission

What to Do If Your Identity Is Stolen

Immediate Action Steps:
  1. Contact financial institutions immediately to close compromised accounts
  2. Place fraud alerts on your credit reports
  3. File a police report to document the crime
  4. Contact the Federal Trade Commission (IdentityTheft.gov)
  5. Change passwords for all online accounts
  6. Document everything - keep records of all communications
  7. Monitor accounts closely for additional fraudulent activity
  8. Consider credit monitoring services for ongoing protection

Prevention Checklist

Monthly Tasks:
  • Review bank statements
  • Check credit card statements
  • Monitor online accounts
  • Organize important documents
Annual Tasks:
  • Obtain free credit reports
  • Review insurance policies
  • Update privacy settings
  • Assess identity protection measures
Remember: Identity protection is an ongoing process. Stay vigilant, regularly monitor your accounts, and act quickly if you suspect any suspicious activity.
